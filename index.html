I am a Cuban Informational Designer who came to New York to expand my professional horizons. Although, being an immigrant in a country is full of challenges, they haven’t stoped me from following my goals to keep growing in the Design field.

Since I was a child, I had artistic inclinations, having a particular preference for painting and photography. I decided to study Visual Communication to allow me to explore different techniques and new creative methods. To my surprise, the range of possibilities that I found inside the profession was beyond my imagination. I discovered inside the digital universe tools that allow me to express myself as an artist and designer, combining different media. After graduating from the Cuban Institute Superior of Design (ISDi), I found another of my passions: teaching. Becoming a professor at the same university where I graduated allowed me to discover that sharing my knowledge pushes me to prepare myself strongly as a professional.

After moving to the United States, I decided to pursue the career of Design and Technology at Parsons The New School for Design. This step changed my life, not only because I changed the direction of my career, but because I’ve been surrounded by people from different nationalities and amazing stories opened my mind to new possibilities.

As I grow, I had become a detail-oriented person, passionate about every single project where I am involved. My profession defines me as the person that I am today. Being an immigrant has made me more sensitive, guiding me to explore topics entirely new for me.
